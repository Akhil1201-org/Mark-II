Getting Started

Learn the basics of using Mark II bug bounty platform

Mark II is a blockchain-powered bug bounty platform that connects security researchers with companies looking to secure their smart contracts and applications.

Companies post bounty programs with encrypted smart contracts, researchers submit encrypted vulnerability reports, and payments are automatically processed using cryptocurrency when bugs are verified.

Click the "Start now" button on the homepage and choose whether you're a security researcher or a company. You'll need to provide an email address and connect a cryptocurrency wallet.

For enhanced security, we recommend enabling two-factor authentication during the signup process.

Researcher accounts can browse and participate in bug bounty programs, submit encrypted reports, and receive cryptocurrency rewards.

Company accounts can create bounty programs, upload smart contracts for testing, review submissions, and pay out rewards to researchers.

Bug Bounties

Everything about participating in and creating bounty programs

Browse active programs on your dashboard or use the search function to find bounties that match your expertise. Programs are categorized by type (smart contracts, web apps, DeFi, etc.) and reward levels.

You can also set up notifications to be alerted when new programs in your areas of interest are posted.

A good report should include: a clear description of the vulnerability, steps to reproduce the issue, potential impact assessment, and recommended fixes.

Include screenshots, code snippets, or proof-of-concept if applicable. The more detailed and actionable your report, the better your chances of reward.

We use a standard severity classification: Critical (complete system compromise), High (significant security impact), Medium (moderate security risk), and Low (minor issues or recommendations).

The final severity rating is determined by the company after reviewing your submission, which determines the reward amount.

Payments & Rewards

Understanding how payments work on the platform

We support major cryptocurrencies including Ethereum (ETH), USD Coin (USDC), Tether (USDT), Dai (DAI), and Bitcoin (BTC).

Most payments are processed on the Ethereum network, but we're expanding to other blockchains for lower transaction fees.

Once your vulnerability report is verified and accepted, payment is automatically processed within 24-48 hours.

The exact timing depends on network congestion and the specific cryptocurrency being used for the payout.

Mark II charges a small platform fee (typically 3-5%) which is deducted from the total reward amount. This covers platform maintenance, security, and payment processing.

You'll always see the net amount you'll receive before submitting your report.

Security & Privacy

How we protect your data and maintain platform security

All vulnerability reports are encrypted end-to-end using advanced cryptographic techniques. Only the company that posted the bounty can decrypt and read your report.

Reports are stored on a distributed system with no single point of failure, ensuring maximum security and availability.

We have a multi-step resolution process: first, direct communication between researcher and company is encouraged. If unresolved, our mediation team reviews the case.

For complex disputes, we may bring in external security experts to provide independent assessment of the vulnerability and its severity.

Technical Issues

Troubleshooting common technical problems

First, ensure your wallet extension is installed and unlocked. Try refreshing the page and clicking connect again.

If issues persist, check that you're on the correct network (Ethereum mainnet) and that your wallet has sufficient ETH for transaction fees.

Ensure your files are in supported formats (.sol, .js, .py, .vyper, .rs) and under the 10MB size limit per file.

Try clearing your browser cache, disable ad blockers temporarily, or try a different browser if the upload still fails.
